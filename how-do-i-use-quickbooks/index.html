    How Do I Use Quickbooks?

    QuickBooks is an all-in-one financial software solution for small business owners. It tracks income and expenses, prepares billings and payroll, organizes clients, and more.

    How Do I Use Quickbooks?

    Like any new piece of software, it requires learning the jargon and the steps to becoming proficient before it can effectively manage all your small business accounting.

    1.Install QuickBooks on your computer. At the prompt, indicate what type of install you want, Custom or Network Options. If you’ll be using QuickBooks on just one computer and it doesn’t share files with other computers, choose “I’ll be using Quckbooks on this computer.” If other computers will access the files, choose the appropriate option for your business to use network sharing. Then indicate the folder where you want the program to install.

    2.Gather important financial papers needed to set up your QuickBooks files. Needed items include your business name and address, tax ID numbers and financial statements.

    3.Create your company file. Click on the QuickBooks desktop icon to launch the program. During the first use, you’ll be given the option to use the EasyStep Interview wizard to walk you through the process of setting up your company file. If you’ve never used QuickBooks, take advantage of the wizard option. View the interactive tutorials as well to learn more.

    4.Set up and manage your vendor accounts. Vendors are businesses you hire to support your business, such as suppliers or independent contractors. Click on “Vendor Center” in the top toolbar. Choose “New Vendor” button to create a vendor account. To add a transaction click on “New Transaction.” QuickBooks can also link to Excel if you’d like to export your vendor information, or to Word if you’d like to draft a letter to your vendors. Click “Excel” or “Word” to complete these actions.

    Last steps

    5.Set up and manage your employee accounts. Click on ““Employee Center” to create and manage employee accounts. Choose “New Employee” to add employee information. Click on the “Transaction” tab to add paycheck and other transactions. Use the “Enter Time” option to manage time sheets.

    6.Create and manage customer accounts. Unlike Vendor and Employee accounts, which involve your expenses, customer accounts serve to track your income and money owed to you. Click on “Customer Center” and then “New Customer and Job” to add a new source of income. Click on “New Transaction” to add transactions such as invoices, estimates and payments. Like the vendor accounts, you can export customer information to Excel or generate letters through Word.

    7.Generate reports through the “Report Center.” Use this feature to get a snapshot of financial areas of your business such a Profit and Loss statement, accounts receivable report or budgets.
